I am **Juana Pola** *(Tehuantepec, 1994)*. My photographic practice is an autobiographical and documentary exercise born from the need to question the environment I inhabit and the territory that situates me. Through my work, I reflect on family, the body, motherhood, gender, and neurodivergence.

My lens is a tool of registration and resistance. I investigate cultural identity and human rights, always seeking to capture the cracks of the everyday to convert them into profound visual narratives. For me, photography is not just an image; it is a constant bond with the stories that shape us.

![Juana Pola](/assets/images/juana-pola-3.jpeg)

## Artistic Journey

My training has been constant in key spaces for photography in Mexico, such as the **Manuel Álvarez Bravo Photographic Center (CFMAB)**, the **San Agustín Arts Center (CaSa)**, and the **Image Center (Centro de la Imagen)**. My work has been exhibited in both solo and collective shows in **Mexico, the United States, Chile, and Paraguay**.
